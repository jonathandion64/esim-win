Frequently Asked Questions

Which eSIM is the cheapest for Europe?

SimOptions is consistently the cheapest, with Europe eSIMs starting at $9.90 for 5GB over 30 days. That's significantly cheaper than Airalo ($18.50 for 5GB/7 days) or Holafly ($19+ for 7 days). SimOptions achieves this through its marketplace model, which creates price competition between multiple carriers.

Do any eSIMs include calls and SMS for Europe?

Yes — both SimOptions and International-SIM offer plans that include calls and SMS. International-SIM's plans from 20GB include unlimited calls & SMS within Europe plus a French phone number. Airalo and Holafly do not offer calls or SMS on any of their European plans.

Is Holafly really unlimited?

Technically, Holafly offers "unlimited" plans, but they have a fair-use policy that is not prominently disclosed on their main pages. In our testing and based on widespread user reports, speeds are throttled after 1–2GB of usage within a single day. In some cases, connectivity dropped entirely after hitting the daily soft limit. This makes Holafly's "unlimited" claims misleading for heavy data users.

Which eSIM has the best 5G for Europe?

International-SIM (powered by Bouygues Telecom) offers 5G on every plan across 34 European countries. As a direct carrier partner — not a reseller — they provide carrier-grade 5G speeds. SimOptions also offers 5G on select plans through Bouygues Telecom and Orange networks. Airalo and Holafly do not offer 5G for European plans.

How quickly does an eSIM activate?

Both SimOptions and International-SIM deliver the QR code instantly by email after purchase. Installation takes 2–3 minutes. The eSIM activates automatically when you land in a covered country — you don't need to do anything extra. Plans are valid for 1 year from purchase, so you can buy well in advance.
